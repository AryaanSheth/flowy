capabilities

What it does

  • Push-to-talk Hold any global hotkey, speak from any app — email, code editor, browser, terminal. Text streams into the focused window as recognition partials arrive, then finalizes when you release. improved in 0.8.1
  • Voice punctuation Say period, comma, new line, or new paragraph and Flowy inserts the matching punctuation or line break.
  • Native recognition Apple SFSpeechRecognizer — built into macOS. No warm-up time, no model files. on-device only
  • Guided setup First-launch wizard walks you through microphone, speech, and accessibility permissions step by step. Done in under a minute.
  • Custom dictionary Word substitutions applied after every transcription. Teach Flowy your names, abbreviations, and jargon. Test them live in the settings panel.
  • Lightweight Under 2 MB. Zero CPU when idle. Sits silently in your menu bar until you need it. Starts at login.
getting started

Up in four steps

  1. 01

    Download

    Click the download button. A .dmg disk image opens. Drag Flowy into your Applications folder.

  2. 02

    Open it

    Flowy isn't signed with an Apple Developer certificate yet, so macOS will block a regular double-click.

    Right-click Flowy → Open → Open
    You only need to do this once. Every launch after that is normal.
  3. 03

    Grant permissions

    The setup wizard walks you through three permissions: Microphone, Speech Recognition, and Accessibility. Each takes one click.

    guided by Flowy
  4. 04

    Speak anywhere

    Focus any text field. Hold your hotkey. Speak. Words appear as you talk.
